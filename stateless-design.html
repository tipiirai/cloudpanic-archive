Stateless design

It's not about JavaScript versus Ruby versus PHP. It's about being stateless. The key to infinite scalability. Simple as that. Tue, 23 Nov 2010 10:00:00 +0200

Tweet

  • No state

    Being stateless means that user information is not store into server's memory. Each request is independent of each other. The client request can be passed to any of your servers randomly.

  • Server up. Server down.

    With the stateless design new servers can be popped up and down on demand. On peak hours you may have 10 servers running serving millions of users and on slow hours 2-3 servers can do the job.

  • Little or no work

    Be stateless right from the beginning. There is no additional work. You are just ready to scale when needed. As a purist you want to be static and build services.
